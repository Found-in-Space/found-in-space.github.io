The Hertzsprung-Russell (HR) diagram is not a map of space, but a map of physics.
It plots a star's surface temperature (on the horizontal axis) against its
luminosity or absolute magnitude (on the vertical axis).

When stars are plotted this way, they do not fill the diagram randomly. Instead,
they form distinct groups. The most prominent is the main sequence, a long
diagonal strip where most stars (including the Sun) spend their lives.

The HR diagram allows astronomers to quickly see how different types of stars
are related and how they evolve over time. It is essentially a census of the
stellar population, showing us the relative numbers of main-sequence stars,
red giants, and white dwarfs.
