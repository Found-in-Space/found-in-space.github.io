In the night sky, stars look like points of white light. But if you look closely—or
through a telescope—you will see that they have distinct colours. Some stars are
clearly blue, while others are yellow, orange, or deep red.

These colours are not just for decoration; they tell us about the star's
temperature. Just as a piece of metal glows red when it is hot and turns white
or blue as it gets hotter, stars follow the same physical law.

Blue stars are the hottest, with surface temperatures of 20,000°C or more. Red
stars are the coolest, with temperatures around 3,000°C. Our Sun is a middle-of-the-road
yellow-white star, with a temperature of about 5,500°C.
