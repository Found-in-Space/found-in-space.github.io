In the context of a galaxy 100,000 light-years across, our Sun's neighbourhood is an
extremely small volume. But even "nearby" in space is huge in human terms.

The closest star system to our Sun is Alpha Centauri, about 4.37 light-years away.
The nearest single star, Proxima Centauri, is slightly closer (4.24 light-years).
If you could travel at the speed of a modern spacecraft (like Voyager 1), it would still
take about 70,000 years to reach those closest neighbours.

When we talk about the "solar neighbourhood," we usually mean the stars within 100
to 200 light-years. This area—our "local bubble"—is what the Gaia mission is currently
mapping with incredible detail.
